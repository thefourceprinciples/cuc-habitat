# Study 005 — Digital Causal Body Without Robot Body

## Aim

Determine whether a digital system could satisfy causal-body conditions without a physical robot body.

## Core question

Can a persistent digital environment create enough boundary, feedback, consequence, and self-maintenance for causal-body candidacy?

## Finding

Yes in principle, if the system has persistent boundaries, continuous input, consequential action, self-regulation, autobiographical continuity, and perturbation robustness.

## CUC interpretation

A digital causal body is not a metaphor for a body; it is a bounded process that must preserve itself under real constraints.

## Falsifier

If the system's apparent identity exists only during isolated sessions and actions do not reshape future state, the causal-body claim fails.

## Status

Conceptual architecture study.
