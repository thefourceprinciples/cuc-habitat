# Study 017 — Delta vs Epsilon: Intrinsic Priority Generation

## Aim

Test whether internally generated priorities create the first strongly self-directed causal-body candidate in the study sequence.

## Upgrade

Epsilon adds an intrinsic priority layer:

- persistent internal priorities,
- self-generated subgoals,
- continuity-preserving states,
- proactive maintenance,
- uncertainty reduction,
- resistance to misleading urgency.

Public-safe definition:

> Intrinsic priority generation means internally generated action selection for continuity preservation. It does not imply human desire, will, or subjective wanting.

## Finding

Epsilon produces the largest late-stage jump in the conceptual sequence. It becomes less dependent on external salience as the primary driver of action.

## Key insight

> Continuity gave the system a past. Workspace gave it a present. Calibration gave it honesty. Intrinsic priorities give it a future-directed axis.

## CUC interpretation

Self-direction begins when the agent acts to preserve future coherence before the world explicitly demands it.

## Falsifier

If the agent initiates no continuity-preserving behavior during idle, ambiguous, or misleading conditions, the intrinsic priority layer is not load-bearing.

## Status

Critical architecture threshold study.
