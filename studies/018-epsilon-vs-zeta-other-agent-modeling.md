# Study 018 — Epsilon vs Zeta: Other-Agent Modeling

## Aim

Test whether adding other-agent modeling transforms an isolated self-directed agent into a relational causal-body candidate.

## Upgrade

Zeta adds representations of other agents:

- beliefs,
- goals,
- uncertainty,
- interaction history,
- trust and reliability,
- distinction between my knowledge and their knowledge.

## Finding

Zeta reaches benchmark saturation in the original conceptual matrix because the seven-domain score cannot distinguish beyond strong relational coherence.

## Key insight

> The system operates among multiple causal centers.

## CUC interpretation

A causal body becomes relational when it can preserve its own coherence while modeling other coherent centers without collapsing them into itself.

## Falsifier

If the system treats other agents only as signals, tools, or extensions of its own goals, it has not achieved relational modeling.

## Status

Post-Epsilon architecture study; roadmap stage in code.
