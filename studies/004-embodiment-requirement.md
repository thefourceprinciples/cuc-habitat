# Study 004 — Embodiment Requirement

## Aim

Assess whether embodiment is required for digital causal-body candidacy.

## Core question

Must an artificial system have a physical robot body, or can embodiment be understood more generally?

## Finding

Embodiment appears important, but body should be interpreted structurally: bounded self-maintenance, environment coupling, and consequential action. A physical robot body is one possible implementation, not the only possible form.

## CUC interpretation

A body is a constraint-bearing boundary through which a process persists, acts, receives feedback, and incurs consequence.

## Falsifier

A purely text-reactive system with no persistent boundary, world coupling, or action consequence does not satisfy the embodiment requirement.

## Status

Conceptual embodiment study.
