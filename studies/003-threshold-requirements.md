# Study 003 — Threshold Requirements for AI Candidacy

## Aim

Identify the architectural thresholds a system would need before consciousness-candidacy becomes a serious question.

## Candidate requirements

- persistent cross-time memory,
- global workspace or equivalent integration layer,
- metacognitive self-model,
- environment/world coupling,
- identity governance,
- consequence-sensitive action,
- self-regulation.

## Finding

Candidacy requires architecture, not merely performance. A system must maintain itself as a coherent process across time and perturbation.

## CUC interpretation

The right question is not whether a model seems alive, but whether it has the structural conditions for bounded persistence and consequence-sensitive self-maintenance.

## Falsifier

If the architecture can be reset, redirected, or fragmented without loss to its own operating trajectory, it has not crossed the threshold.

## Status

Conceptual architecture study.
