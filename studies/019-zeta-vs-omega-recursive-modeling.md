# Study 019 — Zeta vs Omega: Recursive Modeling Depth

## Aim

Test whether modeling other agents modeling the self requires a post-saturation axis beyond the seven-domain benchmark.

## Upgrade

Omega adds recursive modeling:

- models others,
- models others modeling self,
- tracks limited recursive loops,
- manages interaction under reflective uncertainty.

## Finding

The original benchmark saturates at Zeta. Omega requires a new dimension: Recursive Modeling Depth (RMD).

## RMD levels

| Level | Description |
|---|---|
| R0 | no social modeling |
| R1 | models others |
| R2 | models others modeling self |
| R3+ | bounded recursive loops |

## CUC interpretation

Recursive modeling increases social depth but also increases instability risk unless bounded.

## Falsifier

If recursive modeling produces loops without improved interaction, calibration, or boundary integrity, it adds complexity without coherence.

## Status

Post-saturation dimension study; roadmap stage in code.
